Health Surveillance at Work — Key Requirements (Summary based on HSG61)

Health surveillance is required under COSHH Regulation 11 where workers are exposed
to substances hazardous to health and there is a reasonable likelihood that an
identifiable disease or adverse health effect may occur.

The purpose of health surveillance is to:
1. Detect adverse health effects at an early stage
2. Provide data to help evaluate health risks
3. Enable action to be taken to prevent further harm

For wet work exposure (prolonged hand immersion, frequent hand washing):
- Baseline skin assessment before exposure begins
- Regular skin checks at intervals of no more than 12 months
- Self-reporting mechanisms for skin symptoms
- Referral to OH specialist if dermatitis or skin changes detected
- Record keeping in accordance with COSHH Regulation 11(4)

Delegation: Skin assessments may be performed by trained OH technicians under
supervision of a qualified OH nurse or physician. The supervising clinician remains
responsible for the quality of the assessment.

Minimum regulatory compliance requires:
- Written policy for health surveillance
- Competent person to carry out surveillance
- Individual health records maintained for 40 years
- Outcomes fed back to the risk assessment process
